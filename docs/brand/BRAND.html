Logo mark

Two equals, a single phone.

The mark reads two ways. Seen as a monogram it's an E+Q locked into a square. Seen as a symbol it's two equals-lines stacked — the "even" of EvenQuote. Works as a favicon at 16px, as a sticker at 2", and as an app-icon rounded square.

A. "Even" bars — literal, scalable, universally legible. Default primary mark.

B. "=Q" ligature — literal "even quote". Slightly clever. Use for merch/stickers.

C. Quote + even — quotation marks on top, equal bars below. Narrative-forward.

Recommendation: Ship with A as the primary mark. It's the cheapest to render at every size, reads unambiguously as "even", and pairs with the Fraunces wordmark without fighting it. B and C are retained for merch/editorial applications where more personality is welcome.

Palette

Three colors. No gradients.

Ink, cream, lime. The palette is deliberately constrained — any additional hue competes with the accent and dilutes the editorial tone. Grays are produced via ink-with-alpha, not new tokens.

Voice & tone

Direct. Literary. Never cute.

Yes

  • Short sentences. One idea each.
  • Concrete nouns over abstractions ("movers", not "service providers").
  • Em-dashes welcome. Semicolons welcome.
  • Confident numbers when they earn it: "under 24 hours", "$9.99 flat". Avoid hard-coding batch sizes ("up to 20", "up to 10") in customer-facing copy — they age the moment the engine tunes. Prefer "we call local pros" over a number.
  • AI caller introduces itself plainly: "I'm with EvenQuote."

No

  • No exclamation marks outside errors.
  • No "revolutionize", "seamless", "best-in-class", "game-changer".
  • No emoji in product copy. (Internal Slack is fair game.)
  • No fake urgency ("limited time!") — we charge the same flat $9.99.
  • No corporate we-speak. If a human wouldn't say it out loud, rewrite.

Domain & email setup

DNS checklist for evenquote.com.

You own the domain — now lock it down. The webhook magic-link flow sends mail from a @evenquote.com address, so proper SPF/DKIM/DMARC records are required or emails will land in spam. Everything below goes into your registrar's DNS panel.

1. Pick an email provider

  • Google Workspace — $7/user/mo. Best UX. Good for antonio@ and support@.
  • Fastmail — $3/user/mo. Simpler. No Google lock-in.
  • Proton Mail — $4/user/mo. If you want e2e.

Separately — transactional mail (magic links, receipts) goes through Resend or SES from a noreply@evenquote.com sender. Don't send transactional from your human inbox.

2. Records you'll create

TypePurpose
MXInbound mail (from Workspace/Fastmail/Proton).
TXT (SPF)Authorizes your senders. One record, merged.
TXT/CNAME (DKIM)Signs outbound mail. One per sender (Workspace, Resend).
TXT (DMARC)Tells receivers what to do with failures.
CNAMEwww.evenquote.com → Vercel.
AApex evenquote.com → Vercel IP.

3. SPF starter (merge senders into one record)

Only publish one SPF record per domain. If you add Resend later, edit the existing record — don't create a second. 

@   IN  TXT  "v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com include:amazonses.com ~all"

4. DMARC starter (monitor first, enforce later)

Start with p=none and a reporting address so you can see who's sending as you. Tighten to p=quarantine after 2 weeks of clean reports. 

_dmarc  IN  TXT  "v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@evenquote.com; fo=1"

5. Mailboxes to reserve on day one

  • antonio@evenquote.com — your personal.
  • support@evenquote.com — already referenced in the success page.
  • noreply@evenquote.com — transactional sender (Resend/SES).
  • dmarc@evenquote.com — alias that forwards to you; reports land here.
  • hello@evenquote.com — press/partnerships catchall.
  • legal@evenquote.com — Stripe/Supabase require this in ToS forms.

Usage — quick rules

The five things to not screw up.

  1. Wordmark is one word. "EvenQuote", never "Even Quote" or "Even-Quote".
  2. Never set the wordmark in Arial/Inter/Helvetica. Always Fraunces. If Fraunces can't render, fall back to the monochrome SVG.
  3. Lime is an accent, not a background for body copy. AA contrast fails fast.
  4. Mark + wordmark together only when space allows. At sub-32px, use the mark alone.
  5. Capitalize "EvenQuote" internally in code (package name evenquote is fine; user-facing copy is EvenQuote).
