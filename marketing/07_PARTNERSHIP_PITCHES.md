# Partnership Outreach — Cold Pitch Templates

For when paid ads are too expensive and organic is too slow. Partnership channels are where solo founders punch above their weight.

Every pitch follows the same shape: **what you do · what they get · what they have to do · proof you're real**.

---

## 1. Apartment Leasing Offices (Move-in handoff)

**Why this works:** Every new tenant needs a moving company AND often a deep-clean before move-in. Leasing offices field this question constantly. If you can save them 5 minutes per move-in, they'll happily hand out your QR.

**Subject:** `Free service for your incoming residents — would love your team's eyes on it`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{leasing_manager_first_name}},

I run EvenQuote — a $9.99 service that calls local moving companies on a tenant's behalf and emails them a clean comparison of quotes. Took me a year to build because I was tired of telling friends to "just call 5 movers and pick the cheapest."

I'm reaching out because your incoming residents probably ask your team for moving company recs — and I'd guess you'd rather not be the source of those recs (liability + time).

Could I drop off a small stack of QR-coded flyers for your move-in packets? No cost to you, no commission, no tenant data shared with you. The QR points to a EvenQuote landing page just for {{property_name}} residents (priced at $9.99, same as everyone else — no upcharge).

If it helps: I'm a solo founder out of San Marcos. EvenQuote operates as Hocusbuzz LLC. Real human, not a startup with a sales team.

Happy to drop off in person if you're nearby, or mail them. Either way.

— Antonio
{{phone}}
{{evenquote.com}}
```

**Targeting:** Property managers / leasing agents at 100–500 unit complexes. The smaller they are, the more flexible. Skip REITs and big management companies for month one.

**Realistic conversion:** 1 in 10 will say yes. That's enough — one yes = 50–200 incoming tenants/year.

## 2. Realtors (Buyer-side handoff)

**Why this works:** Buyer's agents close, then their client immediately asks "what movers do you recommend?" Most agents have one or two referrals they personally vet. Few have a tool.

**Subject:** `Solving the "who do I use for movers?" question your buyers ask`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{realtor_first_name}},

I saw your listing on {{address}} just closed — congrats.

Quick pitch: I built EvenQuote to fix the question every buyer's agent gets right after closing — "who should I use for movers?" Today most of you either give the same one or two referrals (which gets old for the contractor and the client) or shrug.

EvenQuote is a $9.99 concierge that calls 5 local movers on the buyer's behalf and emails them a clean quote comparison within an hour. Buyer stays anonymous (no contractor lead-spam after), gets real numbers, picks one. We don't take any commission — just the $9.99 from the buyer.

Could I send you a small handout (digital or printed) you could pass along to closed buyers? No cost. No data shared back. If it makes your post-close handoff slightly less painful, mission accomplished.

— Antonio
EvenQuote (Hocusbuzz LLC, San Marcos)
{{phone}}
```

**Targeting:** Independent agents and small teams. Find them on local brokerage sites, Zillow Premier Agent profiles, RealEstate.com.

**Realistic conversion:** 1 in 20. Realtors are inundated with cold outreach. Differentiate by being human and specific to their listing.

## 3. Local Service Pros (Contractor / Cleaner / Handyman side)

**Why this works:** Contractors hate Thumbtack/HomeAdvisor charging per dead lead. EvenQuote sends them ONLY warm jobs (the customer already paid us $9.99 to find them). It's a free supply-side acquisition.

**Subject:** `Free leads (real ones) for {{business_name}}`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{contractor_first_name}},

Found {{business_name}} on Google for {{vertical}} services in {{city}}. Real quick:

I run EvenQuote — homeowners pay us $9.99 to call 5 local pros and get them a quote comparison. The catch for them: they stay anonymous until they pick one. The benefit for you: every call you take is from someone who already paid to get a quote. Not a tire-kicker.

Cost to you: $0. We don't charge per lead, per job, or per anything. (We're funded by the $9.99 the homeowner pays.) You take a 3-minute call from us, give us a real number, hang up. If they pick you, they call you direct.

I'd love to add {{business_name}} to our directory for {{city}}. If you're in, just reply with:
1. Your direct line for quote calls
2. Service area (zip codes you cover)
3. Anything you DON'T do (e.g., "no piano moves")

Real human here — happy to chat by phone if it'd be faster.

— Antonio
EvenQuote (Hocusbuzz LLC)
{{phone}}
```

**Targeting:** Local moving + cleaning + handyman + lawn-care businesses on Google Maps within 25 mi of San Diego (then expand).

**Realistic conversion:** 1 in 5 will reply, 1 in 8 will join. Higher than other partnership pitches because you're offering free supply, not asking for a favor.

**Why this matters double:** Better supplier coverage = lower coverage_gap rate = lower refund rate = better unit economics. This pitch is also operational, not just marketing.

## 4. Local Press (San Diego launch hook)

**Why this works:** Local outlets love the "solo founder builds X to fix Y" story. They get a content piece, you get backlinks + credibility + a smattering of local-search traffic.

**Subject:** `Local SD founder shipping AI tool to fix the "phone-tag-with-movers" problem`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{reporter_first_name}},

I'm a solo founder in San Marcos who just shipped a thing called EvenQuote — a $9.99 service that uses AI voice agents to call moving and cleaning companies on a homeowner's behalf and emails back a quote comparison.

The angle I think might be interesting for {{outlet_name}}:

• Built solo over the last year, no funding, no team
• Local — operating as Hocusbuzz LLC out of San Marcos
• Sits at the intersection of two stories you've covered: AI applied to consumer services, and the "lead-gen tax" that platforms like Thumbtack/HomeAdvisor charge homeowners and contractors
• Real product — happy to demo live or give you a free run for any quote you'd find useful

Not asking for a feature; asking if there's a 2-min phone call where I could pitch the angle and let you decide whether it's a fit. If not, no worries.

— Antonio
EvenQuote
{{phone}}
{{antonio's_personal_email}}
```

**Targeting:** Voice of San Diego, Times of San Diego, NBC 7 SD digital, KPBS digital editor, San Diego Magazine, Path to Pillars (local Substack). One outlet at a time. Don't blast.

**Realistic conversion:** 1 in 30. Press is brutal. The win is the one feature, not the ratio.

## 5. Substack / Local Newsletter

**Why this works:** Local newsletters (Path to Pillars, San Diego Today, etc.) often have a "small business spotlight" or sponsored slot for under $200/month. Smaller audience than mass media but extremely high-intent local readers.

**Subject:** `Sponsoring {{newsletter_name}} — interested in a local-founder spotlight?`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{newsletter_owner_first_name}},

Big fan of {{newsletter_name}} — was reading your {{recent_issue_subject}} edition this morning.

I run EvenQuote, a small SD-based startup ($9.99 concierge that calls movers/cleaners on a homeowner's behalf). I'd love to either sponsor a single issue or be featured in a "local founder" segment if you do those.

Budget: I can do up to $200 for a single placement. Happy to write the copy myself or have you do it — whatever you prefer for fit.

Let me know what makes sense.

— Antonio
EvenQuote
```

## 6. Repeat-customer / referral program (built-in, not outreach)

Not a cold outreach pitch — a system. Build this into the product so every customer who's happy becomes a referral channel automatically.

**In the win-back email** (`05_EMAIL_SEQUENCES.md`, sequence #5):

> Refer a friend, you both get $5 off your next request. Your link: evenquote.com/r/{{referral_code}}

**On the results-delivered page** (after they download their comparison):

> Loved this? Send a friend a free first request. Your link: [Copy]

**Tracking:** Just a `referral_code` column on `quote_requests` and a counter on `profiles`. Cheap to build, compounds the same as SEO.

---

## Cadence and tracking

Per week:
- 5 leasing office pitches (10 min each = 1 hr)
- 5 realtor pitches (10 min each = 1 hr)
- 10 contractor pitches (5 min each = 50 min)

Spreadsheet columns: `recipient_name, organization, pitch_type, sent_date, response_status, follow_up_date, outcome`. Use Google Sheets, not a CRM.

Reality check: 50% will not respond. Don't take it personally, don't follow up more than twice, move on. The 10–20% who say yes are the ones that move the needle.
