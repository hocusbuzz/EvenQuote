## Organic Reddit / Community Comment Templates

These are for replies to existing threads where someone is venting about quote-getting. Never lead with the link. Lead with help. Mention the product only when it's the obvious answer to their question.

### Template A — when someone says "Thumbtack/HomeAdvisor was awful"

> Yeah, that's the lead-gen-marketplace experience by design — they sell your number to 5–10 contractors and you get smashed with calls for two weeks. Few options:
>
> 1. Use Yelp + manually call the top 5 yourself (slow but you stay in control)
> 2. Ask in this sub or your local sub for direct rec — gets you names, not quotes though
> 3. Disclosure: I built a small thing called EvenQuote that does the calling for you for $9.99 and never sells your contact. Happy to comp the fee for anyone in this thread who wants to try it before recommending — DM me.
>
> Whatever you do, don't put your real number on Thumbtack again.

### Template B — when someone asks "what should a 2-bedroom move cost in [city]?"

> Honest answer: there's no one number — depends on distance, day of week, stair flights, and how the company prices (per-hour vs per-job). For [city] in 2026 I'm seeing $400–$900 for a typical 2-bdrm under 20 miles, day-of-week being the biggest swing.
>
> If you don't want to call 5 movers to triangulate, you can either Yelp-call them yourself or use something like EvenQuote (full disclosure: I built it) that calls on your behalf for $9.99 and sends you a comparison. Either path beats getting four contractor sales calls a day from one Thumbtack form.

### Template C — when someone asks for cleaner recs

> Personal recs from neighbors usually beat any directory — what subreddit/city are you in? I can probably ping a couple names.
>
> If nobody bites with names, the workaround is calling 4–5 yourself or using EvenQuote (disclosure: my project) — $9.99 to have us call for you and get 5 real quotes, no spam afterward. But if you can get one good name from a neighbor that's almost always better.
